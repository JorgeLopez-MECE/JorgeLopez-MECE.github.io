About Me

Why Engineering

Aerospace · Oil & Gas · Energy · Manufacturing

I grew up the youngest of a family where three of my brothers were engineers. Long before I understood what that meant, I wanted to be one too. I'd tell my teachers, my friends, anyone who asked — not because I knew what engineers did, but because of what I saw them as. To me, engineers were the people quietly making everything work. The backbone of society.

That feeling never went away. If anything, it sharpened into two things I've always cared about: making a real impact, and finding better ways to do things. Those two ideas have followed me into every internship, every project, and every problem I've taken on.

"I didn't just want a career in engineering. I wanted to actually do something with it."

I'm currently a mechanical engineering student at the University of Houston with a 3.76 GPA and an active Public Trust clearance through NASA. Outside of school, I've been doing the work — optimizing aerospace facilities, supporting energy infrastructure projects, and building tools that make engineers' lives easier.
